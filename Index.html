VisiTrash was conceived on February 1-3rd of 2019 as a weekend Make-A-Thon project. Our mission was to reduce waste on an island resort by engaging with the public during the disposal of their waste in specially designed trash receptacles.

Big Problem - We live on a small planet that is drowning in waste and pollution, at the same time climate change is displacing people and animals. What can we as students do to protect our environment?

Vision - Reducing the amount of single use packaging and food waste is a simple and effective way to help protect wildlife and divert extra food to hungry people. Engaging a large number of people at the time and place where they are throwing away waste is an efficient way to educate and change behavior one interaction at a time.

Why now - Awareness of global warming, ocean plastic pollution and mass extinctions is growing worldwide as our window to contain the damage shrinks daily.

Why us - Only a small, Agile, multi-disciplinary team focused on quickly delivering a financially viable, end to end solution can hope to succeed in this technologically and socially challenging space.
